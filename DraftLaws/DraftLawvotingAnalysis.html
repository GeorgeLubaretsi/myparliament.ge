Analysis of votes per person

Each of the 396 votes for laws in our data set has a document attached to it which indicates for each MP whether he or she was present and how he or she voted. We present a small analysis of these votes per MP. A total of 36778 lines of information is present in all these files, but not all of it is presented in a uniform format. For our analysis, we only use the 27828 number of lines for which we have the following information present:
  1. First name, surname, function and pary affiliation of the MP, and
  2. whether the MP registered for the particular vote, and how he or she voted.
All laws in our data set were passed and obtained a majority vote. We have a closer look at the voting results. First we look at what types of votes were recorded and how often.

Analysis of votes per law

From the 396 votes for laws in our data set, 386 stated that some MP's were absent. On average, 62 MP's were absent at a vote. The maximum number of absent MP's was 101, the minimum was 25.
The average number of MP's supporting a law in our data set is 86. The maximum was 114, the minimum was 52.
Of the 396 votes for laws in our data set, 386 stated that MP's voted against. In 312 of these 0 MP's voted against the law. Of the 80 laws that at least one MP voted against, the average, maximum and minumum number of votes against the law were 5 , 33 and 1, respectively.
